## 1. Introduction
[cite_start]Researchers spend a significant amount of time reading papers, yet it is a skill rarely taught[cite: 122, 123]. [cite_start]This guide outlines the **Three-Pass Method**, a practical and efficient approach to reading research papers and performing literature surveys[cite: 124, 125].

## 2. The Three-Pass Approach
[cite_start]Instead of reading from start to finish, the paper should be read in up to three distinct passes[cite: 137]. [cite_start]Each pass builds on the previous one to achieve specific depth[cite: 138].

### Pass 1: The Quick Scan (5–10 Minutes)
[cite_start]**Goal:** Get a "bird's-eye view" of the paper and decide if it's worth further reading[cite: 142, 143].

1.  [cite_start]**Read carefully:** Title, abstract, and introduction[cite: 145].
2.  [cite_start]**Browse headings:** Read section and sub-section titles; ignore everything else[cite: 146].
3.  [cite_start]**Read conclusions.** [cite: 147]
4.  [cite_start]**Scan references:** Mentally check off ones you already know[cite: 148].

**The Five Cs (Questions to answer after Pass 1):**
* **Category:** What type of paper is this? (Measurement? Analysis? Prototype?) [cite_start][cite: 150, 151]
* **Context:** Which other papers is it related to? [cite_start]What theoretical basis is used? [cite: 152]
* [cite_start]**Correctness:** Do the assumptions appear valid? [cite: 153]
* [cite_start]**Contributions:** What are the main findings? [cite: 153]
* [cite_start]**Clarity:** Is it well-written? [cite: 154]

---

### Pass 2: Grasping the Content (Up to 1 Hour)
[cite_start]**Goal:** Understand the paper's thrust and supporting evidence, but ignore deep details like proofs[cite: 163, 172, 173].

1.  [cite_start]**Examine visuals:** Look closely at figures, diagrams, and especially **graphs**[cite: 165]. [cite_start]Check if axes are properly labeled and if error bars are present[cite: 166].
2.  [cite_start]**Mark references:** Note relevant unread references for future background reading[cite: 168].
3.  [cite_start]**Summarize:** You should be able to explain the main point of the paper to someone else[cite: 173].

[cite_start]*If you still don't understand after this pass, you may choose to set it aside, read background material, or move to Pass 3[cite: 180].*

---

### Pass 3: Deep Understanding (1–5 Hours)
[cite_start]**Goal:** Virtually re-implement the paper to fully understand its innovations and hidden failings[cite: 183, 184].

1.  [cite_start]**Re-create the work:** Attempt to virtually implement the paper using the same assumptions as the authors[cite: 183].
2.  [cite_start]**Challenge assumptions:** Identify and challenge every assumption in every statement[cite: 185].
3.  [cite_start]**Think about presentation:** How would *you* present these ideas differently? [cite: 186]
4.  [cite_start]**Future work:** Jot down ideas for future research based on the paper's findings[cite: 188].

---

## 3. Doing a Literature Survey
Use the three-pass method to map out a new field efficiently:

1.  **Find 3–5 recent papers:** Use Google Scholar or CiteSeer. [cite_start]Perform **Pass 1** on each[cite: 196, 197].
2.  [cite_start]**Find key papers:** Look for shared citations and repeated authors in the bibliographies[cite: 200]. [cite_start]These are the "pillars" of the field[cite: 201].
3.  [cite_start]**Identify top conferences:** Visit the websites of key researchers to see where they publish[cite: 202].
4.  [cite_start]**Scan proceedings:** Browse the latest proceedings of those top conferences to find high-quality related work[cite: 204, 205].

## 4. Summary Table of Reading Depth

| Pass | Time Spent | Focus | Outcome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pass 1** | 5–10 mins | Structure, Abstract, Conclusions | General idea, "The 5 Cs" |
| **Pass 2** | ~1 hour | Figures, Core Arguments | Grasp of content, summary |
| **Pass 3** | 1–5 hours | Assumptions, Proofs, Details | Deep insight, identify flaws |
